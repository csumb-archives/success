Success Stories ~ Meghan Lewis
Student promotes benefits of studying abroad
In 2004, Meghan Lewis's experience as an exchange student in Florence, Italy, exposed her to different cultures and enabled her to make friends in other countries. And it may have advanced her academic career.

"It changed my life-that's why I'm planning to go back. It also makes you view the United States very differently," she said.

Lewis, a native of Grass Valley and lifelong resident of California, majored in Human Communication with an emphasis in pre-law. She was attracted to CSUMB because of its small classes, its diversity, the Human Communication major, and its proximity to the ocean.

Lewis's interest in international affairs led her to pursue a semester abroad. "There are tons of programs available. Anywhere you want to go, you can go. But you've got to find it yourself; no one is going to knock on your door."

The California State University International Programs allow students to study at campuses in 19 countries and pay the same tuition they would pay in California. "But it ended up being more expensive for me because, while tuition is the same, rent and food are more expensive due to the exchange rate," she said.

The school she attended was housed in a villa-"old, run-down, and definitely not glamorous." It was a CSU campus, not an Italian university. "I studied European Union politics, Italian literature-Dante and Machiavelli-and Italian politics." Her professors were mostly English-speaking Italians.

"CSUMB was very helpful and accommodating in working out the transfer of the credits I earned in Italy. I got half my major requirements out of the way," she said.

Lewis prepared for her year in Florence by taking Italian language classes and a course in the history of Western Europe at Monterey Peninsula College during her sophomore year and a class in comparative government at CSUMB.

And what was the biggest lesson she learned?

"I learned how to be respectful of other cultures. We traveled a lot, taking weekend trips to England, France, Germany, Ireland, the Netherlands, Switzerland. And we saw lots of Italy. We took the train, backpacked, stayed in hostels-we didn't have a lot of money. I learned some Spanish, French, and German. And I learned that if I made a little effort with their language, it really pleased people.

"I also learned that I should explore my backyard-California."

Lewis cautioned that overseas study is "not for everyone-you have to be self-sufficient in order to get out of it what you should.

"But I loved it. I can't wait to get back to Italy."

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
Originally appeared on the CSUMB.EDU/news website, December 13, 2005
