Success Stories ~ Pam Johnson
Doin' the "math thing"

When Pam Johnson arrived at CSUMB, she had no idea what she wanted to pursue. In freshman calculus, Pam "got a push from my professor. It just went from there. I'm really into the math thing now."

And the math thing keeps her incredibly busy. During the school year, Pam works as a TA in lower division math classes, helping students and assisting in grading papers. "The last couple semesters I've gotten kind of close with some of the students and they ask me to do private tutoring or go to their study groups."

Last year, Pam was a member of the student team that won the second-highest honor in the National Competition in Mathematical Modeling. She plans to compete again this year-her third and, as a senior, her last chance to win the highest honor. "I'd really like to get that outstanding recognition."

Pam also is a member of the math club, which she confesses is "more social. We got together and talked about nerdy stuff. You see the same people in all your classes, so that makes it a pretty tight community."

At the center of that community is Hongde Hu, Pam's math professor and mentor. "After calculus I took multi-variable calculus, and that's the first class I had with Hongde. In high school, I always liked math and it was my favorite class, but I wasn't great with it. I didn't fully understand the concept. It's great to work with Hongde because in everything we do, he has a way to twist it around and put it in a different perspective so we can understand it. He has all his own proofs-he doesn't look at the book at all. He just shows us everything. I can't imagine doing this with any other teacher."

With Hongde's guidance and scholarship support from the Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation (Pam is of American Indian heritage), the driven 20-year-old senior is now getting a head start on her Capstone project and thinking about jobs and grad school for next year. For the Capstone, she hopes to delve further into probability matrices. "I've just had a little experience with them. I just did a final project for one of my classes on them, but I want to go deeper. I thought they were really interesting. There's so much to it, I just want to learn more about it."

Pam says she wants to get her master's degree in statistics while working as an actuary. "There are companies out there that will start you out with your BA and then pay for you to go get your master's." She thinks her first position may be with an insurance company.

Even with all her involvement in the math thing, Pam finds time to work and pursue her other interests. Growing up in Imperial Beach, just five miles north of the Mexican border, she participated in a marine science program and competed on the swim team. Pam stays close to the water by working the front desk at the Spanish Bay Club in Pebble Beach. There, several club members have asked her to teach swim lessons to their children on the side…and she found a seventh grader in need of some algebra tutoring.

Note: This story was written while Pam was a student. She is now in graduate school at the University of Southern California.

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
