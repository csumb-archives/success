Maria Giuriato
Former HCOM student committed to public service

Maria Giuriato juggles a busy life.

Salinas city councilmember, Health Professions Education Foundation executive director, wife, and mother of two. This accurately describes Maria Giuriato, former Human Communication student.

Giuriato serves as the District 5 councilmember for the City of Salinas and was recently appointed by the governor to serve as the executive director of the Health Professions Education Foundation. She has also worked for the county as community relations manager for the Department of Social Services for the past six years.

"My long term goals are to continue in the political arena and to work in an environment that helps me support members of our society who are struggling against insurmountable odds to escape poverty," she said.

Giuriato is responsible for community and government relations pertaining to legislation, policy and resources. In addition to representing her department to all levels of government, she is directly involved with the community regarding affordable housing, healthcare, education, mentoring, legislation, policy and community engagement. Specific services offered include emergency response to child abuse, elder care and referrals for foster care, emergency benefits, and Medi-Cal.

"Education and knowledge are the keys to addressing injustice in our community," said Giuriato. "This is why I continue to be involved in the education arena and serve as a mentor to several youth every year.

"CSUMB inspired me to continue my life-long goal of providing knowledge and opportunities to people who aren't always in the loop. It also gave me insight into how and why others communicate the way they do. My ethics training has also allowed me the insight to relate to others at a whole different level of reflection and understanding."

Giuriato would like to return to CSUMB in the future for a master's degree. She just needs to find a way to balance the time commitment to further her education-in addition to being a dedicated mom, executive director, and city councilmember.

~ Don Porter, Internal Communication Specialist
