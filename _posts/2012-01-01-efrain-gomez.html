Efrain Gomez '03

An artist since his youth, Efrain Gomez ('03 TAT) has always loved drawing and especially cartooning. Oddly enough, his proposal to create a weekly comic strip morphed into a regular column reviewing movies for Ink Magazine, a weekly entertainment supplement published by the Salinas Californian.

Efrain explains, "I've always loved Calvin & Hobbes and stuff like that, and doing a comic has been always been a goal." During his spare time in college, Efrain created Kindalikesorta.com, a website that showcased his cartoons and animations. When he saw that Ink was getting started, he jumped on the opportunity.

"I contacted Ink to see if they'd be interested in me doing a comic strip, and they said sure, they'd take a local submission and pay me for it. I had two panels, but I never developed a full idea."

Soon after, Efrain went on the job market. He wound up landing a job as a web content specialist at Trend Micro, an internet security company based in Cupertino, but while perusing the classifieds he noticed another opportunity.

"I saw an ad saying Ink needed a movie critic, so I got back to them and said, how about a movie review instead of a comic? They seemed interested, so I sent in an application with examples of movie reviews."

Sure enough, he got the gig.

Efrain says the education he received as a Teledramatic Arts and Technology major relates indirectly to his day job at Trend Micro, but his understanding of film history and movie-making techniques definitely informs his movie critiques.

He relates that the biggest challenge is keeping the reviews short and simple. "I've got to get to the point of the story and summarize the core themes and say why I liked it or not in just 380 words. I can't get too heady."

He also must have an opinion. "Movie reviews have to be extreme-you either love it or hate it, but it can't be mediocre," says Efrain. "Sometimes it's hard to feel extreme and I don't want to lie. Fortunately, Ink is pretty open to being random and off topic, so sometimes I just comment on something else and have fun and then say it was an okay movie."

Though Efrain occasionally feels the call of Hollywood, he's committed to maximizing the life he has in Salinas. He still draws cartoons and portraits and posts his work online. He also does videography and graphic design on the side and uses his internet savvy to help friends design and maintain their websites.

"My name means fruitful in Hebrew, so I thought about that and decided that maybe I'm supposed to be fruitful. Sometimes I regret not doing the L.A. thing, but I'm content with what I'm doing here, and that I have a job and my family. Plus, I get to write about movies and share the experience. Hollywood or not, I'll bloom where I'm planted. Wherever I am, I'm going to produce and be fruitful."


~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
