Success Stories ~ Jessica Massey

Lady luck and a lot of hard work landed Jessica Massey an extremely competitive internship under some of the brightest lights on the Vegas Strip-Jessica will spend her summer working at the MGM Mirage Corporation as part of their Hospitality Internship Program.

Five thousand students nationwide applied for the MGM internship program, and of those only 63 were selected. Jessica is one of three interns who will be working closely with the executive director of human resources to write, revise or otherwise update 6,000 job descriptions.

Jessica applied for this opportunity in December 2006 after business professor Suzanne Kroeze forwarded an email to all seniors in the business major encouraging them to submit applications. "In April, she received a phone call from MGM and completed an interview. "I felt confident in my responses, but was not certain of the outcome, knowing how many other deserving applicants there were."

Soon after, Jessica received an acceptance letter and welcome packet from the leadership development department. Professor Kroeze commented, "Jessica approaches opportunities with a sense of adventure and determination. I am pleased that she earned this summer opportunity."

Jessica credits her mother for inspiring her to succeed in the business world. A first generation college student, Jessica learned the value of education watching her mother slowly climbing the corporate ladder without the benefit of a college degree.

"My mother, Melinda, started working as a cashier at a car dealership when I was four years old. Through the next eighteen years, she transitioned to receptionist, cashier, accounts payable/receivable, assistant office manager, and finally office manager for the Sangera Automotive Group, which is the Mercedes, Volvo and Suburu dealership in my hometown [Bakersfield]. It was an extremely long process but now she is very successful and highly respected in that industry. Because it took her so long to climb the ladder, she always reminds me how much more quickly she could have done it if she had started out with a degree and better job experience.

"Being the first one in my family to go to college was very intimidating," says Jessica. "My uncle, who was like a father to me, was diagnosed with ALS (Lou Gehrig 's disease) in 2002, my senior year of high school. It was important for me to postpone my education to support my family during this difficult time. He lost his battle with this disease in 2003 and I struggled to regain the motivation for school."

After her uncle's death, Jessica's family urged her to continue her education. "[They] encouraged me to choose a school with a good community so that I could fully apply myself." This important criterion led Jessica to transfer from Bakersfield Community College to CSUMB in 2004.

Another source of support for Jessica is the ALS community. "My uncle's disease opened my eyes to a community of people in my own hometown who had daily struggles with ALS and no news of a cure. In Monterey, I felt I could lend my time and energy to help ALS patients and their families whenever possible.

"My first year at CSUMB I saw a banner advertising the Walk to D'feet ALS at El Estero Park. At the walk, I met ALS patients and their support systems. Knowing personally how taxing it can be on a family to care for somebody with such a debilitating disease on a daily basis, I knew I had to do more.

"The second year I became a team captain and my small but determined team raised nearly $1000 which went directly to the new satellite clinic at CHOMP and to the home-care service to Monterey County residents.

"Currently I am working with Tim France of the ALS Association Greater Bay Area Chapter as a media chair and walk chair for the October 6, 2007 walk. I look forward to being a part of this organization for the rest of my life."

Jessica's other plans for the future include graduate school and continuing her business career. "My goal is to become a successful businesswoman who can make a difference in the community and to important causes."

With the MGM internship topping an already impressive resume, we'd say her odds are pretty darn good.

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
