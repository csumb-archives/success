Success Stories ~ Olana Sullivan '08

There are many ways to meet CSUMB's language requirement. You can spend time in the classroom and in the WLC language lab, you can test out, or you can immerse yourself if the language and culture as part of a study abroad experience.

Human Communication major Olana Sullivan chose the latter. She took advantage of a partnership between CSUMB and Cabrillo College to travel to Buenos Aires, Argentina, as part of an exchange program with the Universidad de Belgrano.

"I needed to meet the CSUMB language requirement, but I also I wanted to take the opportunity to see a different part of the world," says Olana, who grew up in the Monterey Bay area.  "I also wanted to learn more conversational Spanish." 

She joined a group of thirty students from CSUMB and Cabrillo who spent four weeks over the summer in Buenos Aires. Olana stayed with a host family and took small, intensive language classes at the university each morning. "My class had eight people" she says. "It was one of the largest."

The Universidad de Belgrano, named for the barrio in which it is located, hosts a number of exchange programs in addition to teaching local students, so Olana had the opportunity to meet other students from all over the world.

After class, Olana headed out to explore the city, her Lonely Planet travel guide in hand. "I explored the museums and botanical gardens, and the different barrios and outdoor markets." she says. "You really get to know yourself when you travel abroad."

Her favorite destination was Recoleta, a neighborhood distinguished by French-style architecture and numerous parks, cafes and galleries. "Recoleta has a huge outdoor market with lots of Argentinean arts and crafts, mate gourds and all kinds of Che Guevara stuff," Olana says. "I spent a lot of time just exploring."

At the end of the day, Olana returned to the home of her host family. "Before you go, you fill out a survey that asks things like, 'Do you like pets? Are you vegetarian?' Then they place you with someone who can fill those needs," she says. "I got placed with a really cool family -- a mother and two daughters."

Olana says the accommodations were modest -- just a room with a small bed and a shelf. "Looking back, I grew a lot from not having as many comforts," she says. "I've become more patient and less high maintenance."

For Olana, the highlight of the trip was a weekend excursion to Iguazu Falls, one of several optional add-ons to the program. The falls, among the largest in the world, are located north of Buenos Aires, at the border of Argentina, Paraguay and Brazil.

The excursion started with a tour of Iguazu National Park, where the students hiked out to cliffs overlooking the falls, followed by a rafting trip. "You're actually being sprayed by the water from the falls and going through rapids," Olana says. "It's a big adrenaline rush."

Now, back in California, Olana remains glad to have studied abroad: "I got this experience and perspective outside of the U.S. Iguazu Falls was the most amazing place I've ever been. And I can carry on a pretty good conversation in Spanish. I'm so glad I went."


~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
