Success Stories ~ Bronwynn Lloyd

"When you discover something, you are reconnected to the past," says Bronwynn Lloyd, a student in her final year at CSUMB who will graduate in spring 2008.

While growing up, Bronwynn absorbed her dad's passion for history. Before she left home to attend CSUMB, she chose marine biology for a major, but it didn't take long for her to shift to the archaeology concentration within the Social and Behavioral Sciences major.

"The first archaeology course I took involved me in a dig at Carmel Mission," she said. At that point, she was hooked. Later, she worked with CSUMB Professor Ruben Mendoza on a dig at the Royal Presidio Chapel. "I learned a lot from working on that project. It's a bit of a puzzle, trying to fit the pieces back together, but that's the fun of it."

Spending long hours searching for artifacts isn't easy. "There are times you go out on a dig but don't find anything. It may be discouraging, but it helps us better understand the history of an area. It explains the topography and the kind of environment there was at the time. "

Last year, Bronwynn was hired as a paid intern in the archaeology lab of the district office of the California State Parks Department in Monterey. The job is a perfect fit to continue her work in the field.

"I'll be in England this summer working on two excavations," she said. "Through the Earthwatch Institute in South Shields, I'll be working on the Roman fort there. At the University of Sheffield's West Halton Field School, I'll be working on a Saxon settlement." Sheffield is also the university Bronwynn wants to attend for graduate school.

"History, as it's passed down through the years, can be manipulated and changed. But when you can bring up the evidence that supports history, you bring into focus what actually happened, as opposed to what probably happened."

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
