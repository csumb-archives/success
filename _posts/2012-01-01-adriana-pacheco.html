Adriana Pacheco ('06)
Adriana Pacheco, a senior at CSUMB, has won a William Randolph Hearst/CSU Trustees' Scholarship for 2006-2007.

The award provides $3,000 scholarships to students based on academic and personal achievement, financial need and community service. The winners will be honored Sept. 19 at the CSU Chancellor's Office in Long Beach.

Ms. Pacheco is a business major. She came from Mexico as a child, and spent her youth working in the fields around the family home in Gilroy, sorting and packing cherries, apricots and peaches. After graduating from Gilroy High School, she enrolled at CSUMB. Her ambition to become the first in her family to graduate from college fuels her academic success.

In her service learning classes, she has worked in AIDS prevention and as a tutor for the LUPE organization in Salinas.

More than 400,000 students attend the 23 campuses of the CSU system. This year, 19 of those students were honored with Hearst scholarships. The William Randolph Hearst Foundation established an endowed scholarship fund in 1984 to honor the founder of the Hearst newspaper chain.~ In 1999, the foundation partnered with the CSU Board of Trustees to supplement the endowment with contributions from CSU Trustees and private donors.

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
