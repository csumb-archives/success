--- layout: archive title: Judy Cortes date: 2012-01-01 ---

Judy Cortes, field placement coordinator in the Department of Teacher Education and Spanish-language instructor in the School of World Languages and Cultures, has been awarded a Fulbright Scholar grant to lecture in Chile.

The four-month grant will begin in March 2007 and will allow Dr. Cortes to lecture at the Catholic University of Temuco, a city of more than 300,000 in the southern part of the country. Her topic will be improving curriculum design.

"One of the things that appeals to me about this assignment is the fact that Temuco is a diverse area," Dr. Cortes said. "There's a large indigenous population," referring to the fact that Temuco is the cultural center of the Mapuche Indians, who make up 25 percent of the area's residents.

Dr. Cortes is one of 850 U.S. faculty members and professionals who will travel abroad to 150 countries during the 2006-07 academic year through the Fulbright Scholar Program. Established in 1946 under legislation introduced by the late Senator J. William Fulbright of Arkansas, the program's purpose is to build mutual understanding between the people of the United States and other countries.

The program is sponsored by the U,S. Department of State's Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs. Recipients are selected on the basis of academic or professional achievement and because they have demonstrated leadership in their fields.

Dr. Cortes, a native of Uruguay, joined the university in 1995, handling field placements in the School of Education and teaching Spanish. She holds a bachelor's and a master's degree in Spanish from UCLA, a master's in Educational Administration from San Jose State University, and a Ph.D. in Hispanic languages and literatures, also from UCLA.

While her primary role at CSUMB has been in teacher education, she teaches Spanish classes for the School of World Languages and Cultures.

"My love is teaching," she said. "How can I supervise teachers unless I teach?"

Dr. Cortes has a broad background in education. Before coming to CSUMB, she worked for the Salinas City School District, in bi-lingual education. She's also been on the faculty at the University of Hawaii and Monterey Peninsula College and was part of a team that wrote a Spanish-language textbook used at Monterey's Defense Language Institute for 20 years.

She is the second CSUMB faculty member to win a Fulbright this academic year. Dr. Lorenzo Covarrubias, an instructor in the Division of Social, Behavioral and Global Studies, left in August to spend the year lecturing at the Autonomous University of Sinaloa-Mazatlan, in Mazatlan, Mexico.

~Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
