Maggie Caldwell ('06)
Finding a path between writing and the environment

"I spent all summer after graduation looking at the newspaper want ads and saying, 'Now that I am a college graduate, there are hundreds of jobs I can get,'" says Maggie Caldwell '06.

Maggie knows the difference between having a degree and not having one-she decided to go back to school at age 40. Now, despite all the jobs she's qualified for, Maggie has decided to follow a different path by starting her own business, Aptos Multimedia. Being self-employed enables her to remain very involved in the lives of her two boys, Max, 14, and Sam, 8, as well as work on what she loves.

"I wanted to pursue employment as a writer and I'm also interested in the Monterey Bay environment and environmental science," says Maggie. By majoring in Human Communication and minoring in Earth Systems Science & Policy, Maggie tailored her education to these interests. "[Professor] Rina Benmayor really steered me in the right direction."

In her second semester at school, Maggie found a job on the ESSP internship website that required her to combine her two areas of study. Using her knowledge of environmental science and her writing skills, she helped Watsonville Wetlands Watch develop a curriculum guide for elementary school students.

Today, Wetlands Watch has become one of Maggie's clients. "After I graduated I was hired to help with their middle school curriculum project. It's really cool to take the best of what I got at CSUMB and translate it into something other students and teachers can use."

Maggie has also written articles for the Aptos Times and Monterey Bay Parent, and this spring Ocean Magazine will feature several of her photographs (including the photo of the gull on the left). "This is the first time a big glossy national publication has picked up my work."

In addition to glossy, nationwide media exposure, Maggie has received recognition for her Capstone project. The CSUMB Faculty Senate selected Maggie as one of three students to represent CSUMB at the Twenty-First Annual CSU Student Research Competition in Dominguez Hills.

"My Capstone advisors, Rina Benmayor and Qun Wang, really kept me focused and moving in the right direction," says Maggie.

Her Capstone, titled Habitat of Identities: Oral Histories of the Watsonville Wetlands, analyzes the oral histories of seven people who at one time or another lived on Harkins Slough Road. Through the interviews Maggie explored the way living in the wetlands shaped her subjects' identities. From the start, when Maggie selected her major and minor, through her studies where she found the right internship and completed an exemplary Capstone, to her new career as a freelance writer, Maggie has succeeded in connecting her love of writing and interest in the environment to the work she produces. This ability to bring seemly divergent paths together should serve her well in the future as she continues to find success as a college graduate.

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
