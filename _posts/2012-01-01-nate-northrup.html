Nate Northrup '04

More than 70 soccer players from all over the country traveled to Wilmington, N.C., last April to try out for a single spot on the Hammerheads, the local professional team.

After two days of workouts, Nate Northup earned that spot.

Northup's career began at CSUMB, where he played on the varsity for four years and still holds the record for most career points. After graduating in 2004 with a degree in Teledramatic Arts and Technology, the Northern California native trained with Tres Cantos Pegaso in Spain and with Hammarby IF in Sweden, then played for the Boulder City Rapid Reserve in Colorado.

His experience abroad proved helpful when he found himself in Colorado.

"In Sweden, I didn't know a soul which was pretty difficult. I learned a lot about myself as far as comfort level and also training with players that are better than you," he said. "I came back to the States with a new level of confidence, which I think allowed me to play for the team in Colorado."

Northup's journey to Wilmington came after he read about the team open tryouts on the United Soccer Leagues website and decided to make the trip with friends. The Hammerheads play in the USL Second Division.

"I just tried to play as relaxed as possible and as comfortable as possible and I guess coach saw that or saw something that he liked and I feel really lucky," he says of the experience.

Northup lists his goal in the May 5 match against the Richmond Kickers as a season highlight.

"The goal was definitely 1 in 100. It was a left-footed shot and I hit it just how I wanted to," he said. "Right spot and right time; it was a pretty goal."

And Northup has enjoyed living on the East Coast.

"I have absolutely fallen in love with Wilmington. It has a really good small town charm to it, kind of like Monterey. Hands down, I'd rather play here than anywhere else at this point."

Northup is thinking about the future. He'd like to have the opportunity to play another season for the Hammerheads and would also like to pursue possibilities in USL Division I and Major League Soccer.

"My whole thing is that I want to improve. It's just determination and focus," he said. "I'm definitely always trying to move forward."

~ Joan Weiner, Public Information Officer
