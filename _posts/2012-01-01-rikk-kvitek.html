--- layout: archive title: Rikk Kvitek date: 2012-01-01 ---
Undersea expert creates one-of-a-kind research opportunities for students

Thanks to Dr. Rikk Kvitek, CSUMB professor and leading expert in seafloor mapping, humanity can now more fully explore life underwater and all its secrets. And the people doing the first round of exploring? Many are CSUMB students.

Rikk has raised millions of dollars to build a science program at CSUMB that exceeds what many universities-even those with Ph.D. programs-could ever hope to offer. Aboard CSUMB's specially-equipped research boats the Van Tresca and the MacGinitie, students study marine habitats up and down the west coast. Student research expeditions have even traveled as far as Alaska and Antarctica. "That's the great fun-taking students around the world doing cool stuff," says Kvitek.

Rikk describes their research as "the study of culture, economics, and politics interacting with nature."

But the hands-on education doesn't end when students return to shore. Again, thanks in part to initial funding from a $2.5 million grant co-authored by Rikk, Bill Head, and Beverly Grova, students have an extraordinary learning space on campus: the state-of-the-art Chapman Science Academic Center.

The skills and experience students gain while working with Kvitek often translate into immediate jobs after graduation. "The jobs that students can get right out of school with the skills they have learned at this university are something that most undergraduates are incapable of achieving," says Rikk. Many go into careers in geospatial technologies or geographic information systems with organizations like the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration or the California Department of Fish and Game.

Kvitek received his Ph.D. in Zoology from the University of Washington and became a research associate at Moss Landing Marine Labs. In 1996, he joined the faculty at CSUMB. Since coming to CSUMB, Rikk has raised over $7 million dollars for different programs in marine science-last year alone he brought in 12 grants totaling over a million dollars.

The CSU system acknowledged Rikk's contributions in 2004, making him the first CSUMB faculty member to receive the Wang Family Excellence Award in Natural Sciences. The award is designed to "celebrate those CSU faculty and administrators who, through extraordinary commitment and dedication, have distinguished themselves by exemplary contributions and achievements in their academic disciplines and areas of assignment." Then-president Peter Smith summed it up this way: "What Rikk has accomplished in the short life of this university is nothing short of remarkable. He is committed to students and faculty working together on real research projects. He writes the grants, gets the funding, recruits the students, and does the work."

Because of Rikk's incredibly successful fundraising efforts, large numbers of students are receiving the opportunity to participate in this amazing research-and use the best seafloor mapping technology available. Due to their efforts, one day humanity may have as much understanding of the seafloor as we do the terrestrial areas of the planet. And CSUMB's professor Rikk Kvitek and his students will have paved the way.

~ Ari Grova, Special Events Intern
