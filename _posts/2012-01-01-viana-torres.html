Viana Torres '02

In a high school three miles north of the U.S.-Mexico border, CSUMB alum Viana Torres is making a big difference.

Viana teaches history at Southwest High School, a comprehensive four year school serving the low income communities of Nestor and San Ysidro, just south of San Diego. As a history teacher, Viana is constantly mindful of whose history she is teaching: "I have a responsibility to my students to teach in a way that is inclusive and relevant to their life experiences."

She sites Ira Shor's book, Empowering Education: Critical Teaching for Social Change as giving her a framework to examine the lessons she is teaching-is it history from a male-oriented Eurocentric perspective or a balanced, multicultural perspective?

"I am a firm believer that the curriculum must be relevant to the student," she says. "If not, they will be bored, become uninterested in school, and potentially drop out. My goal is to help break this cycle."

In addition to fostering critical thinking and civic engagement in her history classes, Viana has implemented the Safe Schools Ambassador program, a student leadership program that trains high school students to stop cruelty and violence on campus through non-violent action.

"This past school year, I had the wonderful opportunity to teach a course specifically designed for the ambassadors. When it came time to develop curriculum for the course, I went back to my old CSUMB Service Learning binders for curriculum ideas," says Viana. "I am proud of the work that my students produce in class. In fact, there will be two students from my Safe School Ambassador class who will be attending CSUMB this fall because they were so interested in continuing the learning that occurred while enrolled in the course!"

In 2007, Viana received the honor of being named Southwest High's Teacher of the Year. She is quick to credit her friends, family, and mentors for helping her achieve success. "I would not be teacher that I am today without the wonderful mentorship that I received from key faculty, staff, and fellow classmates. I also definitely owe a tremendous amount of gratitude to my family for their support throughout my college years."

A Human Communication major, Viana was the first person in her family to earn a college degree, and it wasn't easy at first. "I remember feeling so lost and confused during my first few days on campus." However, as Viana connected with faculty and other students, she found what brought her to CSUMB from her home in San Jose in the first place: "the supportive learning environment promised in the Vision Statement."

Drawn in by the campus community, Viana became active in the Service Learning Institute's Summer of Service Leadership Academy and USA Program, M.E.Ch.A, Mujeres de Maiz, and California Mini-Corps. Her involvement in these organizations and activities reinforced her desire to find a career that would create positive change.

"After having witnessed the positive change that can be created in the lives of students within the classroom, I quickly knew that I wanted to become a high school teacher."

Now Viana lives her mission every day. "As a teacher, my goals are to teach history in a transformative manner that encourages the students to become socially responsible community members. My goals for the future are to live a long happy and healthy life in the company of my family and friends!"

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
