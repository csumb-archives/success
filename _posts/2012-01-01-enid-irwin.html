CSUMB librarian helps take the byte out of homework


CSUMB reference librarian Enid Irwin, with help from several students, is making it safer and easier for youngsters to go online for homework help.

Irwin is the project manager for KidsClick, a kid-friendly search engine based at San Jose State University's School of Library and Information Science, where Irwin teaches online classes in addition to her work at CSUMB.

Kidsclick.org is an easy-to-navigate collection of more than7,000 sites that have been carefully previewed by librarians and found appropriate for youngsters - there's no fear of a child inadvertently stumbling onto an adult Web site.

Enter a word or phrase and KidsClick may display only a handful of related Web sites - not the thousands of links you'd get from Google or Yahoo. The sites have been screened for their educational value.

The site is free and non-commercial - meaning there are no ads. And its content is designed to support the school curriculum.

The site gets 60,000 hits per day and is one of the top websites for children's material, said Irwin. After an overhaul of the site - currently under way - Irwin hopes that number will dramatically increase.

The overhaul includes a redesign to make it more appealing to kids in the 9-to-14 age group, a content review and a formal set of standards to determine what's appropriate material for that age group, and "back-end" work, redesigning the way the site functions.

Graduate students at San Jose State are reviewing the content, while CSUMB students are trying to make it more appealing to kids - working on the graphic elements, logos and overall look of the site as well as creating sound and video files for it.

In the future, she hopes to have CSUMB students film story hours and other activities at local public libraries and post the video on the site.

"All of these things will make the web page more appealing to kids, but still have content that supports the curriculum," she said.

"I want to make the site engaging - fun and informative. And I wanted to work with CSUMB students because I know they'll have the guidance and support of their instructors. I've worked with Valerie Landau and Bobbi Long and they're eager for their students to have an opportunity to do real-world stuff," Irwin said.

Irwin has an MBA, then about 10 years ago, she returned to school to earn a master's in library science at San Jose State. That led to positions with Silicon Valley firms Hewlett Packard and Synopsys, and teaching computer classes at community colleges.

A desire to relocate to Monterey brought her to CSUMB, where she completed the Master's of Instructional Science and Technology program. The skills she acquired along the way are all useful in her position as project manager for KidsClick.

"I like working with students from San Jose State and CSUMB, bringing them together. It's a nice synergy. It gives the CSUMB students a chance to work on a real-world project and shows them - by working with the grad students at San Jose State - what graduate school is like," said Irwin.

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
