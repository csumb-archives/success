Success Stories ~ Nicholas Huerta

Nicholas Huerta, a Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation Scholar, has joined Moss Landing Marine Lab's Submersible Capable of under Ice Navigation and Imaging (SCINI) team. The project, the first of its kind, is using a remotely operated vehicle (ROV) that fits through a small hole in the Antarctic ice. The new ROV allows the team to reach locations under the ice that have been too remote or too deep to access previously. The vehicle has navigational, visual, and sonar imagery capabilities that will allow the SCINI team to create maps of these areas. Nicholas is assisting in building the ROV, and in research to better understand the marine science of the polar region. He'll travel to Antarctica this fall and winter, and will be based at the McMurdo Station.

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
