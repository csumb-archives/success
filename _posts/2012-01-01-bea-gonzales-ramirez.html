Bea Gonzales-Ramirez ('97)
An educational whirlwind

Though it seemed unlikely for a while, education has become an important touchstone in Bea Gonzales-Ramirez's life. Bea decided to drop out of high school after her sophomore year. It didn't take long for her to realize that she wouldn't get very far without an education, so she re-enrolled and earned her diploma.

Following high school, Bea did take some time off to raise her family, but in 1992 she decided it was time to get back in school. She enrolled in Galivan College, which led to what Bea describes as "a whirlwind of education." After three years at Galivan, she decided to enroll at the just-opened CSUMB, where she found herself becoming something of a celebrity.

She was selected to be the student speaker at inauguration ceremonies for the new campus, on Labor Day weekend of 1995. Bea shared the podium with President Clinton, then-White House Chief of Staff Leon Panetta, Congressman Sam Farr and other dignitaries.

"That experience changed my life," Bea recalled some years later. "After all the publicity, I felt like a poster child for the university."

Bea majored in HCOM and graduated in 1997. Immediately, she enrolled at Monterey College of Law and spent the next four years earning her J.D., all while balancing her family life with a husband and two kids.

"After graduating from law school, I jumped right into teaching at Heald College, where I stayed four years. As a teacher, I strove to give back the energy that was given to me by my own faculty at CSUMB when I was a student there. When I saw the spark in the eyes of my students that other instructors had seen in me, I nurtured it."

In addition to teaching, Bea continues to nurture students as a substitute teacher and Hollister School District trustee.

She ran uncontested for a school board seat in the fall of 2006, and was allowed to pick anyone she wanted to swear her in. She chose Mike Sanchez, who, as principal of a continuation school she had briefly attended, had helped her turn her life around.

"I'm so thrilled to do this and I'm so happy for Bea and her family," Sanchez told the Hollister Free Lance newspaper. "To be able to see a student stand next to the President of the United States, and then to come back and be a Hollister School District trustee, it's just an incredible feeling."

After the whirlwind, Bea has this advice for students: "Take it all in-the educational experience, the friendship experience, the social experience. Then pick and choose what you want to make you whole once you earn a degree. Enjoy getting an education, but don't forget to enjoy the journey along the way."
