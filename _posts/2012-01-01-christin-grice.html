Christin Grice '07

When applying to college, Christin thought that CSUMB would be a good fit for him. He knew that it was important to embrace multiculturalism prior to entering the work force, and the emphasis on collaborative work seemed like an effective way to learn. After graduating from Westchester High School in Los Angeles, Christin made the move to Seaside and began what has been a mutually beneficial relationship with this university.

Initally, math and writing proved to be obstacles in college, but no academic challenge was insurmountable with all the educational assistance programs available at CSUMB. By enrolling in the Mathematics Review Curriculum offered by the Math Department and the Writing Workshop of First Year Seminar, as well as making regular use of the Academic Skills Achievement Program, Christin successfully developed the necessary skills in math and writing to excel in his major, Social and Behavioral Sciences, and maintained a GPA above 3.0 for his entire academic career.

Christin has also been an active member of the Black Students United (BSU), serving as the vice president, historian, and webmaster. One of his biggest accomplishments while in BSU was working with faculty and staff members to bring the first Black History Month Art exhibit to the CSUMB library. Professor Richard Bains of the MPA department was one of the lenders for this exhibit. "I thought the exhibit was very well done for an initial effort and was very pleased with the respect and care that was given to my pieces," he said.

Christin is also a member of the Social and Behavioral Sciences Association. As a member of that club, he worked to provide sashes, deemed "honor stoles," to the graduating class of 2007. Christin felt it was important to honor the graduates because he will be the first among his siblings to graduate from college and knew many people in the major represented the first generation in their family to graduate. He also felt that one should honor graduating from their major. Christin says "Being the first child to graduate is an accomplishment, and being a proud older brother, I am a mentor to my younger brother and sister. I motivate them by encouraging them to reach their full potential."

Christin plans to attend graduate school, where he will study marriage and family therapy. He has been accepted to John F. Kennedy University in Campbell and Alliant International University in Sacramento. His goals are to become a licensed marriage and family therapist and to work with a wide range of people, including those with behavioral problems and mental disorders. He is truly dedicated to helping people and motivated to become a therapist.
