Sara Bailey '06

Want living proof that dreams do come true for those dedicated enough to pursue them wholeheartedly? See Sara E. Bailey, who has carved out a career for herself in the highly competitive music industry.

"I've known I wanted to work in music for a really long time," says Sara. "I chose CSUMB because they provided a music and recording technology program. Only a few California state schools have it-Chico is one and their program was impacted. At CSUMB, I could do my own thing and have access to the equipment with professional assistance from professors who actively work in the industry."

As a student, Sara made good use of the equipment-she founded Fog Box records and worked with Associated Students to create two albums featuring CSUMB student musicians and other local artists, Unexploded Ordinance in 2005 and the follow-up release Out of Order in 2006.

For her Capstone, Sara took the label to a new level, developing the first full length CD by a single artist, A. Lee, who had tracks featured on the two previous Fog Box releases. "I had a staff of ten who worked diligently to get all the ins and outs of the label ready. For the final Capstone presentation we had an album release party at Monterey Live."

Monterey Live sold out both nights of the release party, making the Capstone a tremendous success by all accounts. Sara also began working as a talent buyer for Monterey Live, which afforded her another insight into the music industry.

Following graduation, Sara took a job with Paradigm, formerly Monterey Peninsula Artists, a booking agency whose roster includes established acts like the B-52's, Chris Isaak, Dave Matthews Band, and the Black Eyed Peas, as well as the Cold War Kids, Brandi Carlile, G. Love & Special Sauce, Apollo Sunshine, and Cowboy Junkies.

"I work as a contract administrator, issuing legal documents for large scale concerts internationally," says Sara. Though she's getting great experience, the job is time consuming and challenging. "I work long hours. I'm what you'd call a workaholic." She also has a side job booking the U.S.-Canada tour of the Bay Area group Chow Nasty.

Her new responsibilities for Paradigm have put future Fog Box releases on hold for the time being. "We've redirected the focus for our artists from recording to touring and playing shows," says Sara. Despite her hectic schedule, Sara plans on reviving Fog Box on an even larger scale in the future.

It's almost all music in her spare time, too. "On weekends I go to concerts and local shows to support our local music scene. I'm still very plugged in to what's happening at Monterey Live." The part that isn't music? "I like to play air hockey at Monterey Lanes-but that's a new development." She also enjoys drinking cappuccinos and solving crossword puzzles with her fiancé Matt Bollwinkel (MPA '05)

As for the future, Sara feels confident. "Anywhere in the music industry is a somewhere I want to be. It is my dream to one day be an agent, but I'll do anything as long as it's in music. I see a lot of room for growth, a lot of potential."

To others who might dream of following in her footsteps, Sara offers this advice: "When you go into a scary major-like a music major where your parents assume it will be hard to find a job-it is a huge relief to be able to land that dream job right out of school. It's pretty phenomenal. But I've seen it happen for a lot of students coming out of CSUMB."

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
