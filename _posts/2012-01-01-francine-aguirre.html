Francine Aguirre ('05)
Star designer

Images of stars popped up around the local area as publicity posters for First Night's New Year's celebration were distributed. The artwork was produced by a CSUMB graduate (and current university employee) and was the result of a partnership between the university and the event's organizers.

For three years, First Night has worked with CSUMB in a competition to develop the art that goes on buttons, posters, and the web to promote the annual New Year's festival.

Students in Professor Bobbi Long's publications workshop find out what it's like to work on a real-world project and First Night gets high-caliber art that helps define its theme.

"Part of the mission statement of First Night is to involve the community, so it was a perfect partnership," said Sue Ann Hillyer, visual arts director for First Night.

The work of Francine Aguirre (who graduated with her B. S. in Telecommunications, Multimedia and Applied Computing in May 2005) was chosen to promote the 2006 festival. Aguirre, a resident of Salinas, is now employed as a graphic artist at the university.

Her winning design is dominated by a field of stars on a blue background, symbolizing the 2006 theme of "Night of a Thousand Stars."

Each December 31 for more than a decade, the nonprofit First Night has staged an afternoon and evening of visual and performing arts throughout downtown Monterey to welcome in the new year.

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
