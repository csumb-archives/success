Success Stories ~ Rusell Welch '07

Love TV shows and movies that make you squirm?

So does Russell Welch, who turned his passion for all things creepy into a career as an independent filmmaker and visual effects artist for the hit TV show CSI: Miami.

A longtime horror and sci-fi fan, Russell discovered the stories of H.P. Lovecraft just before his senior year. "As I started reading his work, it began to make so much sense to me why he is considered the father of modern horror. Two of my favorite horror films, Alien and The Thing, were very heavily inspired by Lovecraft's work, and it shows," says Russell.

Taking his cue from Ridley Scott, Russell decided to create his own film in the vein of Lovecraft. "Two of his stories contained the recurring concept of creatures coming forth from the ocean and characters having to accept an unsettling destiny, so I felt exploring a horror film with an aquatic theme would be exciting and challenging."

Russell spent the next year writing, shooting, and editing his film, The Crimson Robe, as his Capstone project in the Teledramatic Arts and Technology major. The film debuted at the H.P. Lovecraft Film Festival last October and has played in several festivals since. It tells the story of Keith Weller, a young author who has been discovered alone and screaming on a remote island. It's up to Detective Phillips to find out what happened on the island -- was Keith captured by a cult of amphibious creatures, is he a murderer, or has he simply lost his mind?

"One of the biggest challenges in making the film actually started with the writing process," says Russell. "I had a ton of big ideas for this project, but I was making a short film, not a feature. It was tough condensing things down, but it resulted in having more focus on the core elements of the story."

In addition to having too many ideas, Russell also struggled with too much footage. He explains, "The editing process was also very tedious given that we had shot a ton of footage and there were so many different ways you could take it. The opening sequence in the film was actually constructed in the editing room by taking footage we had shot out at Point Lobos during the day and then manipulating it to look like nighttime during a thunderstorm."

But like any true horror buff, Russell knew the monsters were most important. "The moment I saw one of our creature performers decked out in full makeup, body prosthetics, and adornments, I had this huge grin on my face and just knew that we had done something very special.

"Melissa Lieurance, our creature effects lead, really pulled out all the stops and did some fantastic work. I am forever in debt to her, because without her skills and talent this project would have been impossible to do."

Of course, Russell shares credit with the entire crew, as well as his Capstone advisor, Professor Enid Blader, and the staff and faculty in the TAT department. "This project would not have turned out the way it did without them."

When not working on his film projects, Russell stays busy with CSI: Miami. "The most interesting part of the job is pretty much the whole job: getting to do the visual effects for a popular television show."

Russell loves the challenges that every new shot presents. "No two shots are ever the same. Even if the desired result is similar to something we've done before, there's always something unique to the pathway reaching there."

Those thrills keep Russell going, and his plan for the future includes more storytelling and filmmaking. So if you love a good scare, look for Russell's name in the credits.

 

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
