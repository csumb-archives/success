Rachel Perez-Bitan ('99)
TAT alum combines her love of dance with filmmaking

Teledramatic Arts and Technology alum Rachel Perez-Bitan screened her latest documentary at the REDCAT Theater in June 2006. The film, Countdown: Reflections on a Life in Dance, tells the story of post-modern choreographer Rudy Perez. In addition to the screening, the.doc was picked up by 153 PBS stations and shown at several film and dance festivals.

A dancer herself, Rachel studied ballet with Francisco Martinez and modern dance with Bella Lewitsky. In New York, she studied with the Alvin Ailey Company. Then, in Monterey she met Fran Spector Atkins, which led to her working with the Monterey dance company, SpectorDance, for a year. This inside perspective has allowed her to specialize in dance-related films. "Having a dance background and knowing the technique helps me know how to shoot and edit dance. When I've watched other programs, they were always shot from a wide-angle. You don't see the detail, and that's always bothered me. So having the dance background helps me know which parts of a performance to show from closer up."

The Rudy Perez project began in 2003, when Rachel was hired by a production company to research LA choreographers for a huge PBS.documentary on Latino art and culture. Rachel was already familiar with Perez's work, having read about him in the press and taken a class with him once before. "I knew of his reputation as an interesting character and that his work was important."

Rachel went on to produce and direct an eight-minute segment about Perez as part of the documentary. In the process, she realized that his story really required a longer film. For the first time, Rachel teamed up with her father who also works as a producer and filmmaker. "It was fulfilling because we get along so well, but at times challenging because we are close. In the end, it was a very successful collaboration. I think we will definitely work together in future, because the film was such a good result."

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
