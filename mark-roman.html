Mark Roman '00

It's a good thing Mark Roman and Stacy Chin-Roman have a healthy sense of adventure-Mark's Air Force career has taken them halfway around the world to Okinawa, Japan, where he leads a unit of 120 service members at Kadena Air Base. Mark's mission: maintain and manage over $30 million worth of network infrastructure supporting over 8,000 users. Currently deployed in Southwest Asia, Mark is heading a night shift of 26 personnel who respond to communications issues which could affect all aircraft missions flown by U.S. and Coalition members in support of Operation Enduring Freedom and Operation Iraqi Freedom.

As a communications officer, Mark has built on the range of knowledge he acquired at CSUMB. Now, in addition to the network skills he developed as a Telecommunications, Multimedia, and Applied Computing major, he's responsible for a slate of communication mediums, including postal mail, photography, multimedia, cabling, telephone, satellite, radio, and airfield landing systems. He also works on managing the storage, transport, and recording of communications. "I've learned something every day since day one," says Mark.

Mark also utilizes some of the communication, collaboration, and leadership skills he developed through his involvement with CSUMB Athletics. Mark pioneered the role of Sports Information Officer on the campus by first volunteering to track statistics for the basketball team, occasionally writing up game summaries, and maintaining the Athletics website. Later this turned into a part-time job, and today, this role has grown into regular, fulltime staff position.

While busily attending classes, sporting events, and courting his future wife, Mark never envisioned himself embarking on a military career path. However, the idea of joining the Air Force wasn't completely alien. "My family had always been a military one," says Mark. "My father was a pilot in the Air Force and had taken us across the world and U.S. with him. So the idea of joining the military was in the back of my mind when I thought about things after graduating."

Mark and Stacy married in 2000 and soon after began discussing the possibility of an Air Force career. "I knew I would be involved in something bigger than me and that my family could see interesting places that we wouldn't have imagined. I remember it benefited me as a child because I never took things for granted as I grew up. My wife and I were also explorers in a way, so we knew we would make the most out of each stop in the world."

Mark enrolled in the Air Force Officer Training School and began an intensive three month boot camp. Near the end of his training, the tragedy of 9/11 occurred and reinforced his desire to be a part of something bigger than himself.

Stacy leveraged her student work experience at the Defense Manpower Data Center on the former Fort Ord to land a job as a civilian employee with the Department of the Air Force. As an employee at the Air Force Personnel Center and the 18th Wing's Military Personnel flight, she has directly impacted the careers of thousands of military members.

Mark and Stacy have two children, Gavin and Lauren. Stacy recently took some time off from her job with the Air Force to focus on them and prepare the family for reassignment in September 2007.

"I am blessed with this success as an officer not only because of my experience leading individuals in athletic venues and the challenges of being a TMAC student, but also because of the support of my family," says Mark. "CSUMB and the Air Force have made life a wonderful adventure so far."
