Success Stories ~ Ernesto Rojas

Ernesto Rojas loves to run.

The 21-year-old senior from Fresno is a member of the university's cross country team. He recently won the men's open division in the Valley Runner of the Year Series, 15 races that included half-marathons, 5- and 10-kilometer events and a 2-mile race.

His high school coach, and his passion for running, led him to CSUMB.

Bruce Wade, a teacher and coach at Roosevelt High in Fresno, coaxed him into trying out for the cross country team.

"He started talking to me one day in his classroom and asked me to come out for the team. He said, 'Just bring your shoes and shorts and come on out.' So I did," Ernesto told the Fresno Bee.

Some time later, "Coach Wade told me I could go to college without paying. I didn't believe him. I told him, 'How is that even possible?' I come from a low-income family. I didn't believe it until I started seeing the paperwork.

"Coach Wade encouraged me to come to CSUMB. He talked to me about the Vision Statement and how it fit me really well. I decided that CSUMB would be the right school to start my college education. I liked the area and the idea of going to a small school."

Ernesto especially appreciates the one-on-one time he gets with faculty members.

"CSUMB has turned out to be more than I expected. I enjoy the close relationship I have with my professors. I also enjoy having the chance to go out and do community service. CSUMB gives students a chance to get hands-on experience and relate it to your field of study."

And, with hundreds of miles of trails nearby, it's a great place for a runner.

Ernesto's best finish of the season came at the Westmont College Invitational in Santa Barbara, where he covered the 8-kilometer course in 28 minutes, 35 seconds, placing 14th in the field of 70 runners. The Otters finished second in the eight-team competition.

He's majoring in human performance and wellness education and will graduate in the fall of 2007. After earning a teaching credential, he plans to return to Fresno to teach physical education and - following in the footsteps of Wade and Coach Yi Mao, his mentor at CSUMB - coach track and cross country.

"As a student, Ernesto is inquisitive and interested in the science of exercise, things that relate to what he wants to do," said Dr. Kent Adams, one of his professors. "I'm sure he'll be a good teacher. He makes people feel comfortable and relaxed around him; that creates a good learning environment."

In a way, he's already started his teaching career by keeping in touch with runners at Roosevelt High. What does he advise the young men at his former school?

"I tell guys that if I made it, they can do the same thing. I try to tell them that anything's possible if you put in the work."

And the miles.

~ Joan Weiner, News and Public Information Officer
