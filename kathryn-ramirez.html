Kathryn Ramirez ('98)
An advocate for education

Salinas Union High School Board trustee Kathryn Ramirez '98 believes in the transformative power of education. She's experienced it herself, and now she does everything she can to make sure kids in Salinas, California, and the nation hear that message.

"I believe we need to change the way we talk to our kids. We need to say 'when you go to college' not 'if,' and ask 'where are you going to graduate from college' not 'where are you going to go to college," says Ramirez. "I believe education is a right."

Ramirez grew up in Monterey County and attended North Salinas High School. She was an average student, earning mostly C's. Ramirez feels that because she was so average, she was overlooked. "Now, as a board member, I only see the very best and very worst students. The worst when they are in trouble, getting expelled, and the best when they win some sort of award. It's the average kids, the ones in between we need to target. When you are in the middle, you get ignored."

These are the kids Ramirez tries to reach with her story. "I was persistent. I didn't know what I wanted to be, but I didn't give up." After high school, Ramirez attended Hartnell, where, even though she had graduated from high school, she was told she had to take remedial classes. "The school district didn't prepare me. I didn't have the support." It took Ramirez six years to graduate from Harnell with an associate's degree in transfer studies.

Ramirez then enrolled at CSUMB, and suddenly, things changed. "Before CSUMB I was a flower with the bud closed. CSUMB was the water I needed to bloom." At first, Ramirez though she'd major in business, but she soon realized that her pathway was education. "I wanted to be in education because education had saved me," says Ramirez.

She earned her degree in Liberal Studies and is now enrolled in the master's program in education. "At CSUMB, they're not just educating people to become teachers and administrators. It's up to us to decide what area of education we want to be in. We can be in the classroom or on the school board, setting policy."

Ramirez taught for a few years before joining the Salinas Union High School Board in 2003. She is also an executive board member of the California Latino School Board Association. In these roles, Ramirez has become a fierce advocate for California's schoolchildren, working to ensure that the school district effectively prepares students for college. She also spreads her philosophy that education is a right, communicating to students and parents that college is a realistic, and necessary, pathway. She even has a myspace page where she posts comprehensive scholarship information.

"I've worked on the district, city, and state levels," says Ramirez. She recently went to Washington D.C. to lobby Congress. "The congressmen I met with were very sympathetic and hopeful that we can accomplish what needs to be accomplished to be effective in educating the youth of our nation."

There have been challenges, but Ramirez approaches her role on the board in much the same way she approached her education. "Things take time, but I am persistent. I'm not going to give up or change my path. When something is important to me, I'm not going to let it go. Our kids will get what they need to succeed."

~ Liz MacDonald, Senior Writer/Web Editor
